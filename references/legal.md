# OSINT Legal & Ethical Guidelines

## What is Legal OSINT?

**OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)** is the collection and analysis of **publicly available information**. It is legal when:

1. ✅ Information is publicly accessible
2. ✅ No authentication bypass is used
3. ✅ No unauthorized access to systems
4. ✅ Data is gathered from legitimate sources
5. ✅ Terms of Service are respected

## Legal Framework

### United States
- **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)** - Prohibits unauthorized access
- **Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)** - Protects electronic communications
- **Fair Use** - Allows limited use of copyrighted material
- **Public Records Laws** - Government data is generally public

### European Union
- **GDPR** - Regulates personal data processing
- **ePrivacy Directive** - Protects electronic communications
- **National Laws** - Each country has specific regulations

### International
- **Budapest Convention** - Cybercrime treaty
- **National Laws Vary** - Always check local jurisdiction

## What is Legal

✅ **Allowed:**
- Searching public websites
- Using search engines
- Reading public social media profiles
- Checking WHOIS records
- Using public APIs within limits
- Reading public records
- Analyzing publicly posted data
- Searching data breach databases (for defensive purposes)

## What is Illegal

❌ **Prohibited:**
- Hacking or unauthorized access
- Bypassing authentication
- Password cracking
- Exploiting vulnerabilities
- Scraping beyond rate limits (ToS violation)
- Impersonation
- Harassment or stalking
- Using OSINT for illegal purposes (fraud, identity theft)

## Terms of Service (ToS)

### Respect Platform Rules
- **Rate Limiting:** Many platforms limit requests
- **Scraping Policies:** Some sites prohibit scraping
- **API Quotas:** Stay within free/paid limits
- **robots.txt:** Respect robots.txt directives

### Examples:
- LinkedIn prohibits scraping
- Twitter API has rate limits
- Facebook requires user consent
- Google has query limits

## Ethical Guidelines

### The OSINT Code of Ethics

1. **Respect Privacy** - Don't dox or harass
2. **Transparency** - Be honest about your intent
3. **Accuracy** - Verify information before sharing
4. **Proportionality** - Use minimum necessary methods
5. **Accountability** - Take responsibility for your findings
6. **No Harm** - Don't cause damage or distress

### Best Practices

- **Purpose Limitation:** Use data only for stated purpose
- **Data Minimization:** Collect only what's needed
- **Accuracy:** Verify information from multiple sources
- **Confidentiality:** Protect sensitive findings
- **Attribution:** Credit sources when appropriate

## Use Cases

### ✅ Legitimate Use Cases
- **Cybersecurity:** Threat intelligence, vulnerability research
- **Journalism:** Investigative reporting, fact-checking
- **Law Enforcement:** Criminal investigations (with warrants)
- **Business:** Due diligence, competitive analysis
- **Personal:** Background checks (with consent)
- **Research:** Academic studies (with ethics approval)

### ❌ Illegitimate Use Cases
- **Stalking or harassment**
- **Identity theft or fraud**
- **Corporate espionage**
- **Unauthorized surveillance**
- **Blackmail or extortion**
- **Discrimination (hiring, lending)**

## Risk Mitigation

### Protect Yourself
- Use VPN for privacy (not anonymity for illegal acts)
- Respect rate limits to avoid bans
- Keep detailed logs of your methodology
- Get legal counsel for sensitive investigations
- Document consent when required

### Protect Others
- Anonymize PII in reports
- Don't publish sensitive findings publicly
- Warn before disclosing vulnerabilities
- Respect takedown requests
- Follow responsible disclosure

## When in Doubt

**If you're unsure if something is legal:**
1. **Stop** - Don't proceed
2. **Research** - Check local laws and ToS
3. **Consult** - Get legal advice
4. **Document** - Keep records of your reasoning

**Golden Rule:** If it feels like you're crossing a line, you probably are.
